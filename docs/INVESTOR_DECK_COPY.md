## Slide 4: The Solution

### Headline
**Social listening focused on civic taxonomy by tracking real human social keywords**

### How It Works

**1. Multi-Platform Aggregation**  
We ingest posts from Reddit, X, TikTok, YouTube, Bluesky across 9 civic categories and 56 subcategories (Affordable Housing, Childcare, Gun Violence, Voting Rights, Online Behavior, etc.)

**2. Real Human Social Keywords**  
Unlike brand monitoring tools that track corporate language, we classify content using how people actually talk: "my daycare closed," "rent is insane," "can't afford groceries," "kids online safety." Our taxonomy is trained on 1.36M state bills + real social media language patterns.

**3. Civic-Aware AI Classification**  
Our pipeline filters for civic relevance, classifies by taxonomy, analyzes sentiment with civic context, and scores legislative signal strength (0-100)

**4. Intelligence Dashboard**  
Users see trending issues in their city/state, AI-generated briefings written for legislative staff, signal strength indicators (Baseline → Emerging → Active → Urgent), cross-platform validation (is this Reddit-only or also on TikTok + X?), and 311 data correlation when available

**5. Proactive Alerts**  
Track specific issues and get notified when signal score crosses thresholds, daily digest emails for tracked topics, and export-ready briefings for meetings

### What Makes This Hard
- **Civic language is colloquial:** "my daycare closed" needs to map to "childcare facility closure"
- **Sentiment is contextual:** "Finally, someone is doing something about childcare" is positive (praising action), not negative
- **Classification is multi-label:** A post about "can't afford rent because daycare costs $1,800" touches housing AND childcare
- **Geographic inference:** Subreddits (r/Charlotte), hashtags (#CharlotteNC), and profile locations must map to districts

### Talking Points
- We're not just aggregating posts - we're translating citizen voice into the language of government
- Real human keywords: We track "kids online safety" not "digital youth protection policy" - the way people actually talk about issues
- Legislative Signal Score is the key metric: combines volume, velocity, sentiment intensity, cross-platform consistency, 311 correlation, engagement, and geographic concentration
- The system learns: unclassified civic posts cluster monthly and surface as potential new subcategories
