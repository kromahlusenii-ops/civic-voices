# Test: Category Navigation and Exploration

**Persona**: Maya Rodriguez — Civic Researcher & Policy Analyst

**Role**: Research Coordinator at city council office
**Tech Level**: Intermediate
**Patience**: 7/10
**Goals**:
- Quickly find relevant civic issues in different policy areas
- Understand public sentiment on housing, healthcare, and safety topics
- Identify trending concerns for upcoming policy reports

---

## Pre-test

- Start screen recording
- Clear state (cookies, cache, localStorage)
- Open browser to http://localhost:3000/search
- Have notepad ready for observations

---

## Instructions

Read each step out loud before attempting it. Think aloud as you work - this helps reviewers follow along. Mention any confusion, friction, or unexpected behavior.

---

## Steps

### Step 1: View Category Overview
**Goal**: Understand what policy categories are available on the dashboard

**Do**: Look at the main search page and identify the categories displayed

**Think aloud**:
- What categories do you see?
- Are the icons and colors clear?
- Can you tell what each category covers?
- Is there a logical grouping?

**Success**: You can see at least 6-9 policy categories (Housing, Health, Safety, Education, Infrastructure, Environment, Democracy, Economic Development, Online Behavior)

---

### Step 2: Navigate to Housing & Development
**Goal**: Explore housing-related civic issues

**Do**: Click on the "Housing & Development" category card

**Think aloud**:
- Is the category easy to find?
- What happens when you click it?
- What information appears?
- Can you see subcategories?

**Success**: You see a subcategory view showing housing topics like "Affordable Housing", "Homelessness", "Zoning & Land Use", "Gentrification", "Rental Protections", "Public Housing"

---

### Step 3: Review Subcategory Post Counts
**Goal**: Identify which housing issues have the most public discussion

**Do**: Look at the post counts displayed for each housing subcategory

**Think aloud**:
- Can you see how many posts exist for each topic?
- Which housing issue has the most activity?
- Is the information presented clearly?
- Do any numbers surprise you?

**Success**: You can identify the subcategory with the highest post count and understand relative discussion volumes

---

### Step 4: Click a Housing Subcategory
**Goal**: View detailed conversation data for a specific issue

**Do**: Click on "Affordable Housing" subcategory

**Think aloud**:
- What details load?
- Can you see AI analysis/insights?
- Are actual social posts visible?
- Is sentiment information displayed?

**Success**: You see the IssueDetailView with AI briefing, post count, sentiment breakdown, and social conversation feed

---

### Step 5: Navigate Back to Categories
**Goal**: Return to browse other policy areas

**Do**: Find and click the back button or navigation to return to category overview

**Think aloud**:
- Is the back button easy to find?
- Does it take you where you expect?
- Is your context preserved?

**Success**: You return to the main category grid without losing your session state
